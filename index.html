Use of Technology in Classrooms

A Case Study on Improving Learning through Digital Tools

Introduction to SDG 4

SDG 4: Ensure inclusive and equitable quality education and promote lifelong learning opportunities for all.

What is Educational Technology (EdTech)?

The use of digital tools, apps, and platforms to improve teaching and learning.

  • Smart boards
  • Tablets
  • Google Classroom
  • YouTube lessons
  • AI tutors
  • Online quizzes

Why Technology in Education?

  • Makes learning interactive and faster.
  • Accessible anywhere, anytime.
  • Helps visualize complex concepts.
  • Supports individual learning pace.
  • Engages students more effectively.

Global Statistics (2025)

  • 90% of countries adopted online/blended learning post-COVID-19.
  • 70% of schools use digital learning tools.
  • 1 in 3 students in developing nations use mobile phones for learning.

Indian Context

NEP 2020 promotes digital learning:

  • 60% of urban schools use smart classrooms.
  • 25% in rural schools.
  • 12 crore students accessed DIKSHA platform during COVID-19.
  • 2 million teachers trained in digital teaching.

Case Study: DIKSHA Platform

DIKSHA (Digital Infrastructure for Knowledge Sharing): A government app for teachers and students providing free textbooks, lessons, and training videos in 30+ languages.

Over 150 million learners benefited since 2020.

Examples of Classroom Technologies

  • Smart boards
  • Tablets / Laptops
  • E-content (videos, simulations)
  • Online testing (Google Forms, Quizzes)
  • Virtual labs
  • AI-based personalized learning (ChatGPT, Khan Academy AI)

Benefits of Technology

  • Improves engagement & retention.
  • Makes topics visual and easier.
  • Saves teachers’ time.
  • Encourages self-learning.
  • Provides equal access to remote learners.

Challenges & Problems

  • Digital divide and lack of access.
  • Limited teacher training.
  • Overdependence on technology.
  • Distractions and screen time issues.
  • Infrastructure challenges in rural areas.

Survey Findings

Out of 50 students, 40 said smart classes helped them understand better.

“80% felt that technology-based smart classes made learning easier.”

Solutions & Future Plans

  • Provide basic digital infrastructure in every school.
  • Train teachers effectively.
  • Promote open educational resources (OER).
  • Adopt hybrid learning (traditional + tech).
  • Encourage innovation and local language content.

Link with SDG 4

  • 4.1: Quality primary/secondary education.
  • 4.3: Equal access to higher education (online courses).
  • 4.4: Skills for employment (digital literacy).
  • 4.A: Inclusive and effective learning environments.

Conclusion

Technology is transforming classrooms into active learning spaces. With proper training and infrastructure, it can make education more equal, engaging, and effective — helping achieve SDG 4 by 2030.
